Analysis:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  APPROACH COMPARISON:
    LCM DAG (summarization):  100% active retention
    Structural (scoring):     100% active retention
    BM25 vanilla (scoring):   12% active retention

  LCM and Structural achieve comparable retention.
  Both approaches protect high-value content, but via different mechanisms:
    - LCM: summarizes blocks, preserving unique sentences
    - Structural: scores chunks by structural density, evicts low-value

  KEY ARCHITECTURAL DIFFERENCES:
    LCM:        Summarize + store originals. Costs LLM calls.
    Structural: Score + evict. Zero LLM calls, <50ms decisions.

  LCM RETRIEVABILITY (via lcm_grep): 100%
  LCM's immutable store retains ALL originals regardless of compaction.
  This is LCM's core advantage per the paper — eviction-based systems
  permanently lose data, while LCM can always drill down to originals.
